My Hobbies

Outside of my academic and professional pursuits, I enjoy a variety of activities that help me maintain balance, develop new skills, and create lasting memories. These hobbies have shaped who I am and continue to play an important role in my personal growth and well-being.

Rocket League

Rocket League has become one of my favorite competitive games. The combination of soccer and racing mechanics creates a unique and challenging experience that requires both mechanical skill and strategic thinking. What I love most about Rocket League is how it demands quick decision-making, precise timing, and excellent teamwork.

Playing Rocket League has taught me valuable lessons about perseverance and improvement. When I first started, I struggled with basic mechanics, but through consistent practice and learning from mistakes, I've been able to steadily improve my skills. This experience has reinforced the importance of dedication and continuous learning in all aspects of life.

The competitive nature of the game has also helped me develop better communication skills and the ability to work effectively with teammates, even when things aren't going perfectly. These skills have translated directly to my academic group projects and professional work environments.

Why it's important to me: Rocket League provides a perfect balance between fun and skill development. It's a hobby that challenges me mentally while also serving as a great way to relax and connect with friends. The game has taught me that improvement comes through practice, patience, and learning from failures—lessons that apply to both gaming and real life.

Rainbow Six Siege

Rainbow Six Siege is a tactical first-person shooter that has captured my interest for its emphasis on strategy, teamwork, and communication. Unlike many other games, Siege requires careful planning, map knowledge, and coordination with teammates to succeed. Each match feels like a puzzle that needs to be solved through strategic thinking and effective communication.

One of my most memorable experiences with Siege was learning to work with a consistent team. We developed strategies, learned each other's playstyles, and improved our communication over time. This experience taught me the value of building strong team relationships and how effective collaboration can lead to better outcomes.

The game has also helped me develop better analytical thinking skills. Each round requires assessing the situation, understanding the opponent's strategy, and adapting our approach accordingly. These problem-solving skills have been valuable in my finance and accounting coursework, where analyzing data and finding solutions is essential.

Why it's important to me: Rainbow Six Siege challenges me to think strategically and work collaboratively, skills that are essential in both academic and professional settings. The game has helped me become a better communicator and team player, and it serves as an engaging way to unwind while still exercising my mind.

Baseball

Baseball has been a fundamental part of my life for many years, and it continues to be one of my most cherished hobbies. Playing varsity baseball in high school was one of the most formative experiences of my life, teaching me lessons about discipline, dedication, and teamwork that I carry with me every day.

One of my favorite memories from baseball was during a crucial game where we were down by several runs. Instead of giving up, our team came together, supported each other, and fought back to win the game. That experience taught me the importance of perseverance and never giving up, even when the situation seems difficult.

Baseball has also taught me about the value of practice and preparation. The countless hours spent practicing, studying game film, and working on fundamentals showed me that success comes from consistent effort and attention to detail. This mindset has been invaluable in my academic pursuits, where preparation and practice are key to success.

The sport has also helped me develop leadership skills. As I progressed through high school, I took on more responsibility for mentoring younger players and helping them improve. This experience taught me how to communicate effectively, provide constructive feedback, and lead by example—skills that have been valuable in my role as a Certified Trainer at Davanni's.

Why it's important to me: Baseball represents more than just a sport to me—it's a source of discipline, teamwork, and personal growth. The lessons I've learned on the baseball field about hard work, perseverance, and supporting teammates have shaped my character and continue to influence how I approach challenges in all areas of my life. Whether I'm working on a group project, training new employees, or pursuing my academic goals, the principles I learned through baseball guide my actions.
